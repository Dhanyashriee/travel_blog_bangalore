Nandi Hills

A perfect weekend getaway near Bangalore with stunning sunrise views, scenic landscapes, and peaceful ambiance. Located just 60km from the city, Nandi Hills rises to 4,851 feet above sea level. Experience the misty mornings, historical monuments, and adventure activities like paragliding.

Best Time: 5 AM - 6 PM Distance: 60 km from city Activities: Hiking, Photography, Cycling

Bannerghatta National Park

Home to a rich variety of flora and fauna, the park offers safari tours and a close encounter with wildlife. Spread across 25,000 acres, it houses a biological reserve, butterfly park, and rescue center. Get up close with majestic Bengal tigers, lions, bears, and elephants in their natural habitat.

Safari Times: 10 AM - 4:30 PM Entry Fee: ₹260 for adults Best for: Wildlife Photography

Lalbagh Botanical Garden

A horticultural paradise in the heart of Bangalore, Lalbagh is home to over 1,854 species of plants. This 240-acre garden, commissioned by Hyder Ali in 1760, features the famous glass house inspired by London's Crystal Palace. The centuries-old trees, rare plant collections, and the iconic Lalbagh rock make it a must-visit destination.

Hours: 6 AM - 6 PM Entry Fee: ₹20 Flower Show: Republic Day & Independence Day Photo Spots: Glass House, Rock Formation

ISKCON Temple

One of the largest ISKCON temples in the world, this spiritual haven combines modern architecture with traditional Krishna consciousness. The temple complex, inaugurated in 1997, spans 7 acres and rises 17 stories high. Experience the divine atmosphere, stunning architecture, and the famous prasadam meals that attract thousands of visitors daily.

Darshan: 4:15 AM - 8:15 PM Prasadam Timings: 12 PM - 2:30 PM Special Events: Krishna Janmashtami Cultural Center: Daily Programs

Cubbon Park

A green oasis spanning 300 acres in Bangalore's bustling CBD, Cubbon Park is the city's most beloved public space. Established in 1870, it houses historic buildings like the State Library and High Court. The park features bamboo groves, indigenous and exotic botanical species, and numerous walking trails perfect for nature lovers.

Open 24/7 Activities: Walking, Jogging, Cycling Attractions: Seshadri Iyer Memorial, Aquarium Pet Friendly: Yes

Vidhana Soudha

The seat of Karnataka's state legislature, Vidhana Soudha is an imposing example of neo-Dravidian architecture. Built in 1956, this granite edifice stretches 150 feet tall and incorporates elements from Indian temple architecture. The building is particularly spectacular when illuminated at night, making it one of Bangalore's most photographed landmarks.

Viewing Hours: 6 AM - 8:30 PM Illumination: Sunday & Public Holidays Architecture Style: Neo-Dravidian Prior Permission Required for Entry

Essential Travel Tips

Getting Around

Use Namma Metro for quick city travel. Bus services and auto-rickshaws are readily available.

Best Time to Visit

October to February offers pleasant weather. Avoid summer months (March-May).

Local Cuisine

Try the famous Masala Dosa, Filter Coffee, and Bangalore's unique street food.

Culinary Delights

Bangalore's food scene is a delightful mix of traditional South Indian cuisine and modern cafes. From the iconic MTR and Vidyarthi Bhavan to trendy brewpubs, the city caters to all tastes. Don't miss the famous butter masala dosa, benne dose, and filter coffee!

Must Try: Masala Dosa, Bisi Bele Bath Food Streets: VV Puram, Koramangala
